Do You Need to Make a Plan for Your Family’s Future?

If you don’t have an estate plan, there’s no better time to begin planning than right now. While people may erroneously believe this is a matter that only the wealthy, ill, or elderly need to worry about, the truth is everyone should have an estate plan.

If you unexpectedly pass away from a sudden illness or injury without even a will in place, your estate is considered intestate by the courts. This means that your state’s laws for how an estate should be divided when there’s no will in place shall determine which relatives receive inheritances and how much those inheritances may be. A probate judge may also assign a legal guardian for your minor children who you never intended to care for them.

Needless to say, having an estate plan is crucial. Having an experienced attorney like ours at Aria Law PLLC is just as crucial, so reach out to us today to learn more about how we can help!
